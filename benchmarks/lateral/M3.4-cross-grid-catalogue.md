# M3.4 — Cross-grid Heesch-number catalogue

*Cut: 1 May 2026, on commit `b1fe09f` of `main`.*

The deliverable PROPOSAL §5 Layer 4 promised: a catalogue of
analogues to Kaplan's 2022 Hc = 4 polyhex / polyiamond
result, extended laterally onto the (4.6.12) bevelhex and
(4.8.8) octasquare Archimedean grids that heesch-sat
generation-supports but Kaplan did not publish results for.

The two known Hc = 6 monotile (Bašić 2020, `BasicA7`) is a
hand-constructed non-polyform and is not directly comparable.
The Hc = 4 catalogue Kaplan published is the strongest
empirical baseline; this document is its first lateral-grid
extension.

## Headline table

with the indicated Hc value is known to exist. Polyomino /
polyhex / polyiamond rows come from `data/kaplan-2022/`

come from this project's M3.1 + M3.2 + M3.3 sweeps.

## Cross-grid analysis

### Kaplan grids: rank-ordering by Heesch-friendliness

Sort by smallest n at which Hc = 4 appears:

  1. polyhex     (n = 11)
  2. polyiamond  (n = 20)
  3. polyomino   (not found in n ≤ 19)

Polyhex's local geometry — six neighbours per cell, three
distinct rotation classes for a tile placed on a cell — is the
most generous among Kaplan's three grids. Polyiamond has only
two cell orientations (up-triangle and down-triangle) but its
edge-adjacency structure is comparable; the gap from polyhex
to polyiamond at Hc = 4 (n = 11 vs n = 20) is a real
quantitative difference, not just a sweep-range artefact —
Kaplan's polyiamond catalogue covers all of n = 7..24
exhaustively, with only one Hc = 4 shape found.

Polyomino is the most restrictive: Hc = 3 first appears at
n = 17, and Hc = 4 has not been found at any size catalogued
to date. RQ1 of this project ("is there a polyomino with
Hc ≥ 4?") asks whether the polyomino restriction is
fundamental or just an artefact of the search range.

### Lateral grids: where bevelhex sits

heesch-forge's M3.2 finding — Hc = 2 bevelhex polyforms at
n = 9 and n = 10 — places bevelhex roughly between polyhex
(Hc = 2 at n = 6) and polyiamond (Hc = 2 at n = 10) on the
"first n with Hc = 2" axis. The ratio of Hc = 2 to total
shapes through n = 10 is

  - polyhex:    1 / 4 = 25 % at n = 6 (smallest non-tile).
  - polyiamond: 3 / 103 = 2.9 % at n = 10.
  - **bevelhex:    2 / 2,030,583 = 0.0001 % through n = 10.**
  - polyomino:  1 / 198 = 0.5 % at n = 9.

Bevelhex is by far the most "diluted" — the absolute count
of Hc ≥ 2 shapes is small, but the search space they live in
is enormous, so finding them at all required pushing the
sweep two sizes past the n = 7 M3.1 default. The two shapes
catalogued sit far from the Hc = 0 mass.

By analogy with Kaplan's grids: polyhex's first Hc = 3 is
at n = 7 (one above its first Hc = 2), polyomino's first
Hc = 3 is at n = 17 (eight above its first Hc = 2), and
polyiamond's first Hc = 3 is also at n = 10 (immediately).
Bevelhex's first Hc = 3, if any, is therefore plausibly at
n ≈ 10..12 — within reach of the M3.2-followup compute
budget. n = 11 alone takes ~25 minutes of wall on this
hardware; n = 12 ~2.5 hours.

### Octasquare: too small a sweep so far

The M3.3 octasquare sweep covered (s, o) with s + o ≤ 6
(463 shapes) and found Hc up to 1. Either the (4.8.8) grid
genuinely has no Hc ≥ 2 polyforms at small sizes, or the
sweep just hasn't reached them yet. Compare the polyhex
trajectory: at n = 6 polyhex already has an Hc = 2 shape, at
n = 7 an Hc = 3, but it took until n = 11 for an Hc = 4. If
octasquare follows polyhex's trajectory in the
"few-cells-rich-tiling-options" sense, Hc = 2 should appear
by s + o = 7..8. Extending the sweep to s + o = 7, 8 takes
a few minutes of wall.

## What this delivers for M3.4

PROPOSAL §5 Layer 4 deliverable: "Tabulate analogues of
Kaplan's H = 4 catalogue."

This document is that tabulation. The headline finding is
that **bevelhex has Hc = 2 polyforms at n = 9, 10**, the
project's first non-trivial Heesch-number result on a
non-Kaplan grid. Hc = 3 / Hc = 4 analogues on bevelhex and
octasquare remain open; the path forward is a compute-bound
extension of the existing M3.2 / M3.3 harnesses (no new
algorithmic work).

## What's not done

- **Octasquare extension to s + o ≥ 7.** Few minutes of
  compute; would resolve "are there Hc = 2 octasquares?".
- **Bevelhex extension to n = 11 / 12.** ~25 min and ~2.5 h
  respectively; would resolve "are there Hc = 3 bevelhex
  polyforms in the search range Kaplan covered for hex / iamond?".
- **Polyform symmetry analysis.** Kaplan's catalogue does not
  separately tag shapes by their internal symmetry group. The
  Hc = 4 polyhex / polyiamond catalogue might reveal patterns
  if grouped by D₆ / D₃ classes. Out of M3.4's scope.
